# Privacy & HIPAA Policy

Sunrise Dental Clinic is fully committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal health information in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

## HIPAA Compliance

As a covered healthcare entity, Sunrise Dental Clinic adheres to all HIPAA Privacy Rule and Security Rule requirements. Our staff receives annual HIPAA training, and our systems are secured to protect your electronic protected health information (ePHI).

## What Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information to provide your dental care:

- Full name, date of birth, and contact information
- Insurance information and billing details
- Medical and dental history, medications, and allergies
- Treatment records, X-rays, and clinical notes
- Payment information (securely stored)

## How Records Are Shared

Your health information is shared **only** with providers directly involved in your treatment. We do not sell your data. Disclosures to third parties occur only when:

- Required by law (e.g., public health reporting)
- Authorized in writing by you
- Necessary for treatment coordination with a referred specialist

## Your Patient Rights

As our patient, you have the right to:

1. **Access** your records — request copies of your dental records at any time
2. **Amend** your records — request corrections if information is inaccurate
3. **Restrict** certain uses of your information
4. **Receive a list** of disclosures we have made
5. **File a complaint** with our Privacy Officer or the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services

## Requesting Records

To request your records, submit a signed Records Release Authorization form (available at the front desk or via our patient portal). Records are typically provided within **15 business days**. A nominal fee of $0.25/page may apply for paper copies.

## Records Retention

- **Adult patient records** are retained for a minimum of **7 years** from the date of last service.
- **Minor patient records** are retained until the patient reaches age 18, plus an additional **7 years**.
