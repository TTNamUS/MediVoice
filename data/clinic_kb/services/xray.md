# Dental X-Rays

Dental X-rays (radiographs) are a critical diagnostic tool that allows our dentists to detect problems not visible to the naked eye, including cavities between teeth, bone loss, infections, and developmental abnormalities.

## Types of Dental X-Rays

**Bitewing X-Rays**
Show the upper and lower back teeth in one image. These are used to detect cavities between teeth and monitor bone levels. Taken every **1–2 years** for most patients.

**Periapical X-Rays**
Show the entire tooth from crown to root tip and the surrounding bone. Used to evaluate a specific tooth for infection, abscess, or root issues. Taken as needed.

**Panoramic X-Rays**
A single wide image showing all teeth, jaws, sinuses, and the TMJ (jaw joint) in one view. Useful for assessing wisdom teeth, detecting cysts, and general jaw evaluation. Recommended every **3–5 years**.

**CBCT (Cone Beam CT)**
A 3D imaging technology used for complex cases such as implant planning, impacted teeth, or complex root canal cases. Available on referral.

## Digital X-Rays at Sunrise Dental

We use **digital radiography**, which exposes patients to up to **80% less radiation** than traditional film X-rays. The digital sensors capture images instantly, allowing our dentists to review them on screen and zoom in for detailed analysis.

## What X-Rays Detect

- Cavities (especially between teeth)
- Bone loss from gum disease
- Infections and abscesses
- Impacted or unerupted teeth (including wisdom teeth)
- Root abnormalities
- Tumors or cysts (rare)
- TMJ assessment

## Cost

- Bitewing set (4 films): approximately $50–$80
- Panoramic: approximately $100–$150
- Most dental insurance covers diagnostic X-rays at 100%.

## Safety During Pregnancy

Routine X-rays are typically postponed during pregnancy unless there is an urgent clinical need. When X-rays are necessary, a lead apron with thyroid collar is used to protect mother and baby. Modern digital X-ray doses are extremely low — comparable to a few minutes of background radiation from the environment.
