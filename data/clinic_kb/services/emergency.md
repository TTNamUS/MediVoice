# Emergency Dental Care

Dental emergencies can happen at any time. Sunrise Dental Clinic is committed to providing prompt care when you need it most. Dr. Michael Nguyen leads our emergency dental services with same-day appointments and an after-hours emergency line.

## What Qualifies as a Dental Emergency?

The following situations require urgent dental attention:

- **Severe, uncontrolled toothache** — especially throbbing pain that wakes you at night
- **Knocked-out (avulsed) tooth** — time is critical; re-implantation is most successful within 30 minutes
- **Dental abscess or swelling** — swelling of the face, cheek, or gum with or without fever
- **Broken or cracked tooth with a sharp edge** — especially if cutting the tongue or cheek
- **Lost filling or crown** on a painful tooth
- **Dental injury from trauma** — broken jaw, significant bleeding, or multiple teeth displaced

## What to Do While Waiting — Knocked-Out Tooth

If a permanent tooth is knocked out:

1. Pick it up by the crown (white part), NOT the root
2. Rinse gently with clean water — do not scrub
3. If possible, place the tooth back in the socket and hold it there
4. If unable to re-insert, store the tooth in **cold milk** or between your cheek and gum
5. Call us immediately and head to the office — every minute counts

## When to Go to the ER Instead

Go directly to a hospital emergency room for:

- Difficulty breathing or swallowing (airway emergency)
- Significant facial trauma (fracture, lacerations)
- Uncontrolled bleeding from the mouth
- Suspected jaw fracture

The ER can manage life-threatening emergencies; our team handles dental-specific follow-up care after stabilization.
