# Composite Fillings

When a cavity forms, a filling is the most common and effective way to restore the tooth and stop further decay. At Sunrise Dental Clinic, we use **tooth-colored composite resin fillings** for a natural, aesthetically pleasing result.

## Composite vs. Amalgam

Traditional silver (amalgam) fillings have been used for decades, but composite resin offers several advantages:

- **Appearance:** Matches the natural color of your tooth — virtually invisible
- **Bonding:** Bonds directly to the tooth structure, often requiring less removal of healthy tooth
- **Safety:** Free of mercury, though amalgam fillings are considered safe by the ADA
- **Durability:** Modern composites are highly durable and last 7–10+ years with good oral hygiene

We use composite as our standard filling material for all new restorations.

## The Filling Process

1. **Numbing:** Local anesthetic is applied to ensure complete comfort throughout the procedure
2. **Decay removal:** The decayed portion of the tooth is removed using a dental drill or air abrasion
3. **Tooth preparation:** The cavity is cleaned and shaped for optimal bonding
4. **Composite placement:** The resin is applied in layers and sculpted to fit your bite
5. **Curing:** Each layer is hardened instantly using a blue LED curing light
6. **Polishing:** The filling is polished to a smooth, natural finish
7. **Bite check:** Your occlusion (bite) is verified and adjusted as needed

## Cost

- **With insurance:** Most plans cover 80–100% of composite fillings on back teeth; coverage varies.
- **Without insurance:** Approximately **$150–$300 per tooth**, depending on the size and location of the cavity.

## Duration

Most single-tooth fillings are completed in **30–60 minutes**.

## Aftercare

Some sensitivity to hot, cold, or pressure is normal for a few days after a filling. Avoid very sticky or hard foods for 24 hours. Contact us if sensitivity persists beyond 1–2 weeks.
