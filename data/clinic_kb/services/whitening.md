# Teeth Whitening

A bright, confident smile is one of the most sought-after cosmetic improvements in dentistry. Sunrise Dental Clinic offers professional whitening options that are safer, faster, and more effective than over-the-counter products.

## In-Office Zoom Whitening

Our **Zoom! in-office whitening** treatment delivers dramatic results in a single 90-minute appointment:

- A professional-strength hydrogen peroxide gel is applied to the teeth
- A specialized Zoom! LED light activates the whitening gel for three 15-minute sessions
- Results: teeth can lighten by **4–8 shades** in one visit
- **Cost: approximately $400–$500**

This is ideal for patients who want immediate, dramatic results for a special event or simply want the fastest option.

## Take-Home Whitening Trays

For gradual whitening at home, we offer professionally customized take-home tray kits:

- Custom-fitted trays made from impressions of your teeth
- Professional-strength whitening gel (much stronger than store-bought strips)
- Worn for 30–60 minutes per day or overnight, depending on the gel concentration
- Full results typically seen in **2–3 weeks**
- **Cost: approximately $200–$300**

## How Long Do Results Last?

Professional whitening results typically last **1–3 years**, depending on your diet, tobacco use, and oral hygiene habits. Coffee, tea, red wine, and dark berries can re-stain teeth over time. Touch-up treatments and take-home trays can help maintain your results.

## Who Is NOT a Good Candidate?

Whitening agents only work on natural tooth enamel, not on dental restorations. Whitening is **not suitable** for patients with:

- Dental crowns, veneers, or bonding on front teeth (restorations will not change color, creating a mismatch)
- Severe intrinsic staining (e.g., tetracycline staining) — may require veneers instead
- Untreated cavities or gum disease (must be treated first)
- Active tooth sensitivity

## Managing Sensitivity

Temporary sensitivity to cold during and after whitening is common. We recommend using a desensitizing toothpaste (such as Sensodyne) starting one week before treatment and throughout the process. Sensitivity gel included with professional kits provides additional relief.
