# Root Canal Treatment

Root canal treatment — also called endodontic therapy — is a procedure that saves an infected or severely damaged tooth by removing the inflamed pulp (the inner tissue of the tooth), cleaning and shaping the root canals, and sealing the tooth to prevent reinfection.

## When Is a Root Canal Needed?

A root canal is recommended when:

- The pulp (nerve and blood supply inside the tooth) becomes infected or inflamed
- A cavity has progressed deep into the tooth, reaching the pulp
- The tooth has suffered trauma or a crack that exposes the pulp
- A dental abscess has formed (a pocket of infection at the root tip)
- Prolonged sensitivity to heat/cold or spontaneous throbbing pain

## Myth-Busting: Root Canals Are Not Painful

One of the most persistent dental myths is that root canals are extremely painful. In reality, **modern root canal treatment is no more uncomfortable than getting a filling**. The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you should feel little to no pain during treatment. The discomfort most people associate with root canals is actually the *infection itself* — the root canal relieves that pain.

## What Happens During the Procedure

1. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthetic numbs the tooth and surrounding area completely
2. **Isolation:** A dental dam (rubber sheet) isolates the tooth to maintain a sterile field
3. **Access:** A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth
4. **Pulp removal:** The infected or inflamed pulp tissue is removed from the canals
5. **Cleaning & shaping:** Canals are cleaned, disinfected, and shaped using fine files
6. **Filling:** Canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed
7. **Restoration:** A temporary or permanent filling closes the access opening

## Crown Placement After Root Canal

After a root canal, the tooth becomes more brittle due to the loss of its inner tissue. In most cases, a **dental crown** is recommended to protect the treated tooth from fracture and restore full chewing function. Crown placement is typically scheduled 1–2 weeks after the root canal.

## Number of Visits

Most root canals at Sunrise Dental Clinic are completed in **1–2 visits**, depending on the complexity of the tooth and severity of infection.

## Cost

- **Without insurance:** Approximately **$800–$1,500** depending on which tooth is treated (front teeth tend to be less expensive than molars).
- **With insurance:** Most PPO plans cover 50–80% of endodontic treatment after deductible.

## Success Rate

Root canal treatment has an impressive **success rate of over 95%**, and treated teeth can last a lifetime with proper care and a protective crown.
