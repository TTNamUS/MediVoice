# How Often Should I Come In?

**Q: My dentist always says every 6 months, but is that really necessary? What happens if I skip a year?**

A: The every-6-months recommendation exists for very good reasons — and skipping visits carries real risks to your oral and overall health.

## Why Every 6 Months?

**Tartar builds up between visits.** No matter how well you brush and floss, tartar (hardened plaque) accumulates in areas your toothbrush cannot reach. Tartar can only be removed by a dental professional. After 6 months, buildup becomes significant enough to begin damaging gum tissue.

**Cavities grow silently.** A small cavity caught at a 6-month checkup can be treated with a simple, inexpensive filling. The same cavity missed for a year may have progressed to the point of requiring a root canal and crown — a much more costly and time-consuming treatment.

**Early detection of serious conditions.** Every exam includes an oral cancer screening. Oral cancer caught early has a dramatically better survival rate. We also monitor for signs of gum disease, bite problems, grinding, and other conditions that worsen without treatment.

## What If I Skip a Year?

Skipping one year does not guarantee problems, but it significantly raises your risk:

- Gum disease (gingivitis → periodontitis) can progress substantially in 12 months without cleaning
- Cavities that were tiny at month 6 may reach the nerve by month 12, requiring a root canal
- Tartar accumulation accelerates bone loss in gum disease patients

## Some Patients Need More Frequent Visits

Patients with active gum disease, high cavity risk, or medical conditions affecting oral health (diabetes, dry mouth, immune suppression) may be placed on a **3–4 month recall schedule**. Amara Singh will discuss your recommended frequency after each visit.

## The Bottom Line

Two visits per year is the standard for a reason. Preventive care at $100–$150 per cleaning (without insurance) is far less expensive — and far less uncomfortable — than treating problems that develop when cleanings are skipped. Your mouth will thank you.
