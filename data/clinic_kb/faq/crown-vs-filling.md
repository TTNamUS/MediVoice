# Do I Need a Crown or a Filling?

**Q: My dentist said I need a crown, but I thought I just had a cavity. Why can't I just get a filling?**

A: This is one of the most common questions patients ask, and it is a fair one. Both fillings and crowns restore damaged teeth — but they work at different scales of damage.

## When a Filling Is Sufficient

A composite resin filling is the right choice when:

- The cavity or damage is relatively small (less than about 1/3 of the tooth's biting surface)
- Enough healthy tooth structure remains to support the filling
- The tooth has not cracked or fractured
- The damage has not reached the pulp (nerve)

Fillings are faster, less invasive, and less expensive. **Cost: approximately $150–$300 per tooth** without insurance.

## When a Crown Is Necessary

A dental crown (cap) covers the entire visible portion of the tooth above the gumline. A crown becomes necessary when:

- **Large decay** has destroyed more than approximately half the tooth — not enough healthy structure remains to support a filling without fracture risk
- **Cracked tooth syndrome** — a crack runs through the tooth; a crown holds the pieces together and prevents painful splitting
- **After a root canal** — the tooth becomes brittle after pulp removal; a crown protects it from fracturing under chewing forces
- **A very large old filling breaks down** — and the surrounding tooth is too weakened to support a new filling
- **Fractured cusps** — one or more cusps of the tooth have broken off

**Cost of a crown: approximately $1,000–$1,500** per tooth without insurance. Most PPO plans cover 50% of crown costs.

## The Crown Process at Sunrise Dental

1. **Appointment 1:** The tooth is prepared (shaped), an impression is taken, and a temporary crown is placed
2. **Lab fabrication:** Our dental lab creates a custom porcelain crown (10–14 days)
3. **Appointment 2:** The permanent crown is fitted, adjusted, and cemented

Some same-day (CEREC) crowns are available — ask our team if your case qualifies.

## How Do I Know Which I Need?

Your dentist will evaluate the extent of damage using a visual exam and X-rays. If you have concerns about a crown recommendation, you are always welcome to ask for a clear explanation of why a filling won't suffice — we will gladly walk you through the clinical reasoning.
