# Are Dental X-Rays Safe?

**Q: I'm worried about radiation from dental X-rays. Are they really safe?**

A: This is a completely understandable concern, and the answer is a reassuring yes — dental X-rays, especially modern digital X-rays, expose you to an extremely small amount of radiation.

## How Much Radiation Is in a Dental X-Ray?

To put it in perspective:

- A **full set of bitewing X-rays (4 films):** approximately **0.005 millisieverts (mSv)** of radiation
- A **panoramic X-ray:** approximately **0.01 mSv**
- The average American receives approximately **3.1 mSv per year** from natural background radiation (cosmic rays, soil, building materials)
- A transcontinental flight from San Jose to New York exposes you to about **0.03 mSv** of cosmic radiation

In other words, a dental X-ray delivers less radiation than a few hours of being alive outdoors.

## Digital X-Rays — Even Lower Radiation

At Sunrise Dental Clinic, we use **digital radiography**, which reduces radiation exposure by up to **80%** compared to traditional film X-rays. Digital sensors are more sensitive than film, requiring less radiation to produce a clear, high-quality image.

## How Often Are X-Rays Taken?

- **Bitewing X-rays:** Every 1–2 years for low-risk patients; annually for those with a history of cavities
- **Panoramic X-ray:** Every 3–5 years
- **Periapical X-rays:** As needed for specific clinical concerns

We follow the ALARA principle — using the lowest radiation level that achieves a diagnostically useful image.

## Pregnancy Precautions

Routine X-rays are **postponed during pregnancy** unless there is a clinical urgency (such as an infection requiring diagnosis). When dental X-rays are necessary for a pregnant patient:

- A **lead apron with thyroid collar** is always used
- The dose is localized to the mouth and negligible in the abdomen
- Your and your baby's safety is our top priority

If you are pregnant, please inform our front desk when scheduling and remind us at check-in.
