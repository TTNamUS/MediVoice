# Knocked-Out Tooth — Emergency Steps

**Q: My tooth got knocked out. What do I do?**

A: A knocked-out permanent tooth is one of the most time-sensitive dental emergencies. **Act immediately** — the chances of saving the tooth drop significantly after 30 minutes. Follow these steps:

## Step-by-Step: What to Do Right Now

**Step 1 — Find the tooth**
Locate the tooth as quickly as possible. Handle it carefully.

**Step 2 — Pick it up by the crown only**
The crown is the white, visible part of the tooth. Do NOT touch the root — the cells on the root surface are critical for reattachment and are easily damaged.

**Step 3 — Rinse gently if dirty**
If the tooth is dirty, rinse it briefly and gently under clean running water for no more than 10 seconds. Do not scrub, dry, or wrap the tooth in a tissue.

**Step 4 — Try to re-insert the tooth**
If you can, gently place the tooth back into the socket and hold it in position by biting down on a clean cloth or gauze. This is the best-case scenario.

**Step 5 — If you cannot re-insert it, store it properly**
Place the tooth in one of the following (in order of preference):
1. **Cold milk** — the best storage medium
2. **Saliva** — hold it between your cheek and gum
3. **Saline solution** (contact lens solution or salt water)
4. **Last resort:** plain water is better than letting it dry out

**Do NOT store the tooth in tap water for long periods** — it damages root cells.

**Step 6 — Call us immediately and head to the office**
Call **(555) 743-0200** (or **(555) 867-5309** after hours) while someone drives you to our office. Tell us a tooth was knocked out — we will prepare for your arrival.

## Important: Time Is Critical

- **Under 30 minutes:** Very good chance of successful re-implantation
- **30–60 minutes:** Possible but decreasing success rate
- **Over 60 minutes:** Significantly reduced chance of tooth survival

**Note:** These instructions are for permanent (adult) teeth only. Do not attempt to re-implant baby (primary) teeth — call us for guidance.
