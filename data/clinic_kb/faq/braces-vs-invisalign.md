# Braces vs Invisalign — Which Is Right for Me?

**Q: Should I get traditional braces or Invisalign? What's the difference?**

A: Both options can achieve beautiful, straight smiles — but they work differently and suit different situations. Here is a side-by-side comparison to help you decide:

## Comparison Table

| Feature             | Traditional Braces           | Invisalign                        |
|---------------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Appearance**      | Visible metal brackets/wires | Nearly invisible clear trays      |
| **Removable?**      | No — fixed in place          | Yes — remove to eat, drink, clean |
| **Food restrictions**| Yes (no hard/sticky foods)  | None — remove trays before eating |
| **Cleaning**        | More complex around brackets | Easy — remove trays and brush normally |
| **Cost (at Sunrise)**| ~$3,500–$5,000              | ~$4,000–$6,000                    |
| **Treatment time**  | 12–24 months                 | 12–18 months (typical)            |
| **Complexity handled**| All levels, including severe| Best for mild-to-moderate cases   |
| **Compliance required**| Minimal (fixed)            | High — must wear 20–22 hrs/day    |

## When Braces Are the Better Choice

- **Severe crowding or spacing** that requires more significant tooth movement
- **Significant bite correction** (severe overbite, underbite, or crossbite)
- **Younger patients or teens** who may not reliably wear Invisalign as required
- Patients who prefer "set it and forget it" (no worrying about wearing the trays)
- Budget-conscious patients (braces are typically less expensive)

## When Invisalign Is the Better Choice

- Adults and older teens who prioritize aesthetics
- Mild to moderate crowding, spacing, or relapse cases
- Patients with an active lifestyle (sports, instruments) who want a removable option
- Those who want easier oral hygiene during treatment
