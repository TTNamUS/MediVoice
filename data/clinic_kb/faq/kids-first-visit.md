# My Child's First Dental Visit

**Q: When should my child first see a dentist?**

A: The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends the **first dental visit by your child's first birthday**, or within **6 months of the first tooth erupting** — whichever comes first. Early visits help us catch problems early and, most importantly, help your child build a positive relationship with dental care before any issues arise.

Dr. Patel sees patients as young as 12 months old.

**Q: What happens at a baby or toddler's first visit?**

A: The first visit is gentle and short — more about getting comfortable than doing a lot of clinical work:

- Dr. Patel will do a "lap exam" — your baby sits in your lap while Dr. Patel quickly examines the emerging teeth and gums
- We'll talk about teething, thumb-sucking, bottle use, and diet
- We'll demonstrate proper cleaning technique for infant teeth
- No X-rays, no scary instruments — just a quick, friendly look

**How to prepare your child:**

- Talk about the dentist positively beforehand — "We're going to meet the tooth doctor who keeps your smile healthy!"
- Read age-appropriate books about dental visits (e.g., *Berenstain Bears Visit the Dentist*)
- Avoid using words like "hurt," "shot," or "drill" — let Dr. Patel introduce things in her own child-friendly way
- Schedule for a time when your child is well-rested (morning appointments often work best for young children)

**What NOT to say:**
Avoid saying "it won't hurt" (creates anticipation of pain) or "be brave" (implies there's something scary). Keep it matter-of-fact and positive.

**Parent presence:**
You are always welcome to stay with your child during their appointment. Dr. Patel will guide you on the best way to provide comfort without unintentionally increasing anxiety.
